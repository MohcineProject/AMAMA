3. **DLL anomaly — process image in dlllist**: The dlllist entry at L5461 shows `RuntimeBroker.exe` loaded at `0x7ff642560000` as a module. This is actually **normal** — on Windows, the main executable image is always the first entry in the PEB's `InLoadOrderModuleList`. Volatility's `dlllist` plugin enumerates this list and includes the EXE itself as the first entry. This is standard behaviour, not evidence of module unlinking or hollowing. Agent 1's flag here is a false positive on the plugin's normal output.

These are all standard user-context privileges. No `SeDebugPrivilege`, no `SeTcbPrivilege`, no `SeImpersonatePrivilege` enabled. Normal for a medium-integrity RuntimeBroker.

5. **Cmdline**: `-Embedding` is the standard COM activation argument for RuntimeBroker. Completely legitimate.

6. **No malfind, no network, no suspicious DLLs from non-System32 paths** in the evidence (the 3 shown DLLs are all from `C:\WINDOWS\SYSTEM32\`).

**Verdict**: REJECTED. The dll_anomaly signal is a false positive — the EXE appearing as the first dlllist entry is standard Windows/Volatility behaviour.
